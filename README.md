## ğŸ“ˆ Key Insights
The analysis reveals several important findings:
Magnitude plays a critical role in tsunami generation, with most tsunami-triggering quakes occurring above 7.5 on the magnitude scale.
Shallow earthquakes have significantly higher tsunami potential due to the rapid displacement of ocean water.
Tsunami events cluster along coastal subduction zones, particularly where oceanic plates dive beneath continental plates.
Depth variability is higher in tsunami-linked earthquakes, showing a wider distribution compared to non-tsunami events.
Correlation studies show strong positive relationships between magnitude and tsunami occurrence, whereas depth behaves more variably.
Geographic plots indicate that the Pacific Ring of Fire remains the most active and tsunami-prone region globally.
These insights, taken together, strengthen the understanding of seismic conditions that elevate tsunami risk.
